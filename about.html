Vector Grand Prix

A minimalist browser-based racing simulator focused on physics, precision, and the pursuit of the perfect lap.

01 / Mechanics

Physics & Control

Vector GP uses a simplified momentum-based physics model. Acceleration is automatic; your focus is on steering and finding the optimal racing line. The challenge lies in managing your speed through corners—if you carry too much momentum, the friction won't hold you, and the car will drift wide into the barriers.

02 / Competition

Weekly Rotation

Vector GP follows a high-stakes circuit calendar. Every Wednesday at 07:00 UTC, the current track is retired and replaced. Each layout is available for one week before leaving rotation for a full year. Your challenge is to master the geometry and set your personal best before the cycle continues.

03 / Engineering

The Stack

Built with Vanilla JS and HTML5 Canvas. The physics runs at a fixed 60Hz update rate to keep movement consistent across different screens. No frameworks, no engines, just raw code and some math to simulate basic momentum.

04 / Origin

Pen & Paper Roots

This project started as a simple curiosity: what happens if you take the logic of the pen-and-paper game Racetrack and turn it into a real-time experience?
