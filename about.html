About Vector GP

Vector GP is a free browser arcade racing game built for short time trials, fast retries, and weekly tracks.

Genre Browser arcade racing game
Format Short time trials and practice sessions
Controls Keyboard and touch steering
Roots Paper Racetrack game inspiration
01 / Overview

What kind of game is Vector GP?

Vector GP is a browser racing game and arcade racing game focused on short laps, fast retries, and readable controls. The goal is simple: learn the track, clean up your steering, and lower your best lap time without crashing.

02 / Controls

Why does it feel different from other racing games?

Vector GP plays like a minimalist top-down racing game built around momentum. The car accelerates automatically, so the challenge is not throttle control but steering, line choice, and carrying the right speed through corners without over-correcting.

03 / Origin

From paper Racetrack to browser racing

Vector GP takes inspiration from the pen-and-paper Racetrack game and turns that graph-paper-style racing idea into a real-time web racing game. That is part of why it can also appeal to people looking for a paper racing game, a math racing game, or a racing game where geometry and route planning matter as much as reflexes.

04 / Loop

Built for quick sessions and one more lap

Runs are short, retries are immediate, and each mistake is easy to understand. That makes Vector GP a strong lunch-break game and gives it the fast reset loop that players often describe as “one more lap” brain rot in the best possible way.

05 / Rotation

Why the weekly track schedule matters

Every Wednesday at 07:00 UTC, a new circuit is added to the roster. Older tracks stay available, which means the browser racing lineup keeps growing while still giving players a fresh layout to learn each week.

06 / Modes

Two ways to play the same roster

Time Trial

Time Trial is the purest version of Vector GP: one clean lap, one result, and a fast reset loop built around personal bests.

Session

Session mode is for longer runs on the same track. It keeps the steering-first feel of the core arcade racing game while giving you room to build rhythm and consistency across multiple laps.

07 / Play
